Regional climate projections for African countries processed for the national and subnational scale

Online tool - Many non-climate experts have to deal with climate related questions including policymakers, researchers in the fields of agriculture, energy, human health etc. Even though most climate research institutes provide their observations and climate projections to everyone interested, processing and interpreting this data requires a high level of understanding which undermines this apparent accessibility. With this tool we want to provide preprocessed and pre-selected graphics to policymakers and researchers working at the national and subnational level on african countries.

Access the tool on: http://regioclim.climateanalytics.org

Date2017, November 15
 

We created this tool to facilitate the access to state of the art regional climate projections to a wider public. The used data is publicly available and the scripts developed for this website are based on open source code. Our contribution here is to constrain the information to some basic graphics that can easily be interpreted and reused for reports involving climate change.

Projections can be shown for monthly mean temperature and precipitation and for three temperature and precipitation related extreme indicators (Hot extremes (TXx), wet extremes (RX1day) and 5day wet extremes (RX5day)).

Four basic graphics are presented: A map of the climatology, a map showing the projected change, a regionally averaged transient plot and a regionally averaged annual cycle plot.

Climate projections are taken from an ensemble of 4 regional climate models. In all graphics the ensemble mean of the projections is shown. To inform the user about uncertainties in model projections, in the transient plot and the annual cycle plot, we additionally display the model spread. In the projection map, areas where no agreement about the projected change is found are hidden (colored in gray).

By default projected changes in climate indicators are shown as the difference between a specific future time period and the reference period 1986-2005 for a middle-of-the-road scenario (RCP4.5). Alternatively, levels of global warming can be selected instead of future time periods. Available levels are +2°C, +1.5°C and +0.61°C above preindustrial levels, +0.61°C representing the reference period 1986-2005.

Projections can be shown annually for a special month or for custom defined seasons allowing for a more accurate analysis of seasonal features.

The annual cycle and the transient plot can also be shown for subnational administrative regions. Administrative regions can be merged into custom regions to match your regions of interest.











Example: How is climate change going to affect precipitation in Senegal?

As a first overview we look at the map of projected changes in monthly precipitation for the 2040s. A decrease in monthly precipitation is projected for most western regions. Large parts of the map are colored in gray. In these regions the 4 analyzed climate models don't agree on the the change in precipitation.

These changes have to interpreted in the context of the current climatic conditions in Senegal. Looking at the current repartition of precipitation in the country, one observes that the South is generally wetter than the North. Therefore, the precipitation decrease in the already dry North-West might have large impacts.

For some sectors, changes in precipitation might be most impacting for certain periods in the year. For instance, projections about changes in the amount of precipitation during the beginning of the rainy season are of great interest for farmers. The annual cycle shows that the rainy season starts in June/July and ends in September/October. The observed decrease in precipitation is is strongest in June - at the very begining of the rainy season.

As suggested by the previous graph, changes in precipitation are strongest in June and July. Therefore, it is worthwhile to look at regional patterns of projected changes in precipitation for these two months. For the newly defined season (June+July), we observe a country-wide decrease in precipitation which is most pronounced in the south-west.

Instead of analyzing the annual cycle of precipitation for the whole country, we now want to focus on the southern parts of Senegal that are mostly affected by a decrease in precipitation. For this purpose we merge three administrative regions in the South-West of the country: Kolda, Sédhiou and Ziguinchor.

For the selected region, seasonal changes in precipitation have similar features as for the whole countries. As expected, the decrease in precipitation is more pronounced with a significant decrease of 40mm in July.
