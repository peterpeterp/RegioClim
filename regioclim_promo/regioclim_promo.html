Regional climate projections for African countries processed for the national and subnational scale

Online tool - The website creates graphs of regional climate projections depending on the users interest. The publicly available output of regional climate models has been bias corrected and preprocessed to allow non-expert users to work with it.

Access the tool on: http://regioclim.climateanalytics.org

Date2017, November 15
 

We created this tool to facilitate the access to state of the art regional climate projections to a wider public. The used data is publicly available and the scripts developed for this website are based on open source code. Our contribution here is to constrain the information to some basic graphics that can easily be interpreted and reused for reports involving climate change.

Projections can be shown for monthly mean temperature and precipitation and for three temperature and precipitation related extreme indicators (Hot extremes (TXx), wet extremes (RX1day) and 5day wet extremes (RX5day)).


Four basic graphics are presented: A map of the climatology, a map showing the projected change, a regionally averaged transient plot and a regionally averaged annual cycle plot.

Climate projections are taken from an ensemble of 4 regional climate models. In all graphics the ensemble mean of the projections is shown. To inform the user about uncertainties in model projections, in the transient plot and the annual cycle plot, we additionally display the model spread. In the projection map, areas where no agreement about the projected change is found are hidden (colored in gray).

By default projected changes in climate indicators are shown as the difference between a specific future time period and the reference period 1986-2005. Alternatively, levels of global warming can be selected instead of future time periods. Available levels are +2°C, +1.5°C and +0.61°C above preindustrial levels, +0.61°C representing the reference period 1986-2005.

Projections can be shown annually for a special month or for custom defined seasons allowing for a more accurate analysis of seasonal features.





The annual cycle and the transient plot can also be shown for subnational administrative regions. Administrative regions can be merged into custom regions to match your regions of interest.

  • First of all we look at the climatology of the reference period to get an understanding of the different climatic zones of Senegal.
